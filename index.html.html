    Why I Chose Lovely Professional University

    I have decided to pursue my B.Tech at Lovely Professional University (LPU), India, because it offers the perfect combination of high-quality education, modern campus facilities, and excellent career opportunities.

    Here are the main reasons for my choice:

    • Comprehensive Curriculum: LPU provides a well-rounded B.Tech program in Software Engineering that covers both theoretical concepts and practical skills, preparing me for the demands of the tech industry.
    • Experienced Faculty: The university has highly qualified professors and industry experts who can guide me in both academics and career development.
    • State-of-the-Art Campus: LPU has modern labs, libraries, and innovation centers, which will allow me to explore emerging technologies and gain hands-on experience.
    • Strong Placement Record: LPU has an excellent track record of placing graduates in top tech companies with competitive salaries. This aligns perfectly with my goal of securing a high-paying job to repay my ESP loan.
    • Diverse Student Community: Interacting with students from across India and abroad will help me develop teamwork, communication, and leadership skills.
    • Affordability & Loan Support: The tuition fee is reasonable compared to other private universities, and it is compatible with the Bhutan ESP Loan, making financing realistic and manageable.

    Overall, LPU provides the right mix of academic excellence, career prospects, and personal growth opportunities, making it my ideal choice for pursuing a B.Tech degree.

    How I Plan to Finance My Studies

    To pursue my B.Tech in India, I am planning to avail the Bhutan ESP Education Loan. This loan offers a 4% interest rate on the principal (gyelsups), which is quite reasonable, but the repayment will require careful planning.

    Based on the loan amount I need — approximately Nu. 1,000,000/- for the entire program — I anticipate that it could take up to 10 years to fully repay once I land a stable job after graduation. This is a long-term commitment, and I want to be transparent about the financial responsibilities involved.

    The good news is that a B.Tech in Software Engineering in India opens the door to high-paying jobs in the tech sector. Entry-level software engineers typically earn between INR 400,000 – 600,000 per year (~Nu. 480,000 – 720,000), and with experience, salaries grow significantly. This means I can comfortably manage my loan payments while supporting myself, and eventually contribute back to our family.

    While the interest rate is a concern, I believe that careful budgeting, prioritizing my career, and choosing a strong, in-demand field will ensure financial stability. I want you both to know that this plan is realistic and that I am prepared to take on this responsibility.
